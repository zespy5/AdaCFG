Abstract

Classifier-free guidance (CFG) has become the de-facto mechanism for steering diffusion-based image-to-image translation models. However, a single fixed guidance scale is rarely optimal across different source images and target domains, leading to either under-edited or over-edited results.

We propose AdaCFG, a framework that predicts an adaptive classifier-free guidance scale and its velocity schedule on a per-sample basis. Our lightweight guidance predictor is trained with a combination of text-alignment, structure-preservation, and negative-prompt objectives, and it can be plugged into existing editing pipelines such as Plug-and-Play (PnP) diffusion and InstructPix2Pix (IP2P) without retraining the backbone. Experiments on the NuScenes driving dataset show that AdaCFG produces consistently more faithful and structurally coherent translations than fixed-CFG baselines.

Method

Figure 2. Overview of the AdaCFG pipeline.

Given a source image and a target-domain prompt, AdaCFG encodes both with CLIP and feeds the concatenated features into a small MLP that outputs (i) an initial guidance scale ginit and (ii) a velocity term that controls how the scale evolves along the diffusion trajectory. The resulting schedule is consumed by a standard editing backbone.

  • Adaptive Guidance Prediction. Per-sample (ginit, velocity) replaces the fixed CFG scale.
  • Guidance Scheduler. Decreasing / cosine schedules produce the final per-step guidance.
  • Editing Backbone. Compatible with PnP and InstructPix2Pix without any backbone retraining.
  • Training Objective. Combines text CLIP similarity, structural DINO similarity, domain-mean alignment, and a negative-prompt term.
